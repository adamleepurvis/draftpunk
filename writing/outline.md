# The Kedu — Book One Outline

---

## Theme

What you are willing to give, and why, and whether you are honest about the reason.

Four characters. Four different relationships to sacrifice:

- **Jalp** — gave everything in the valley and spent eternity taking it back. The original betrayal of the theme.
- **Enki** — gave incrementally, transactionally, for selfish reasons. Lost himself in the accumulation of costs.
- **Nezha** — gave the thing she always wanted, once, freely, for love. The purest version of the theme.
- **Lanu** — gave the thing she was born with and never had to earn, to stop the man who started it all. Closes the circle Jalp opened.

---

## Characters

### Lanu
Kedu master-in-training in Tulo. Congenital gift — she can hear the bashi without effort or training. Best friends with Nelo, the town bashi. Raised under Huli alongside Nezha. Goes through the New Light Ceremony, gets lost in the valley fog, is found by the bashi leaders who reveal the truth about kakari and the corruption in Estopio. Paired with Hachi. Returns to confront the institution. Sacrifices her memory of Nelo to turn the lian against Jalp. Refuses leadership. Goes home to Tulo as kedu master — able to hear bashi, but no longer remembering the one who prepared her for all of it.

**Arc:** Given everything freely. Has to learn what it costs to give something back.

### Nezha
Kedu master, raised from orphancy by Huli. Devoted to him in a way that has been engineered from the beginning. Jealous of Lanu's congenital gift and her apparent carelessness with it. Meets Koya in the Estopio temple. Falls in love. Watches Huli kill Koya during the reveal. Asks the bashi to heal Koya. Gives up her newly acquired ability to hear them — the thing she spent her life reaching for — without hesitation. Koya lives. Nezha goes home to Tulo with Lanu and Koya, tending the tree without the ability she sacrificed.

**Arc:** Spent her life devoted to the wrong person, wanting the wrong thing. Gives up the right thing freely the moment she has it.

### Enki
Born into poverty in Estopio's Quag with the gift. Exploited by a system that conscripts the gifted into service of wealthy families. His sister had the gift, tried to connect to the Great Kedu without a bashi, lost herself — alive but unreachable, housed in a Rings sick hall. Enki wants structural change but for the wrong reasons — anger and revenge rather than justice. Pushes further into the tree's presence than he names at the ceremony, paying costs he stops counting. In the valley, encounters Jalp's presence in the lian — a voice that knows exactly what he wants and offers him the one thing no one else can: a way to recover his sister. He thinks he found an ally. He has found the thing that has been living in the lian for generations, waiting for someone exactly like him. Jalp's promise was never real. Enki gets what he wanted — the system shifts — and loses himself in the getting of it.

**Arc:** Legitimate grievance, wrong reasons, wrong guide. The tragedy of a man who was right about everything except what it justified.

**Misdirection note:** Opening scene makes him feel like the protagonist. One planted detail — *he passed a woman struggling with a child on one hip and a basket twice her size on the other, thought briefly that he should help, and kept moving* — reads as relatable on first pass, damning on second.

### Kaol (J'Rasa)
Born into one of Estopio's powerful Ring families. Has the gift. Has been sneaking below the Rings for years — to the sick hall, to Eflo, to a baby named Yu. Approached by the Sakoku Society, who want him to plant a destructive seed in the valley during the ceremony. Refuses. His best friend Syca, already compromised by the Society, plants it instead — quietly, almost accidentally. Kaol provides testimony that dismantles the Ring families, including Syca's. Syca disappears from his life without a confrontation. Kaol gives up his family's position. Stays in Estopio. Sets up a sick hall in the Rings. Eflo comes to help. Yu is there, older, learning to talk.

**Arc:** Privilege confronting itself and choosing to dismantle rather than reform. Loses the person he loved most because of a choice he made correctly.

### Nelo
Tulo's bashi. Vulpine. Chose Lanu — when and why we never see. Has been watching Huli for a decade and can tell exactly two people: one is the source of the wrongness, one is a child she's been quietly preparing. Sends Lanu to the marsh the morning she dies. Doesn't look back. Her ears fold over in resignation. Everything she carried, alone, for ten years, becomes the thing Lanu eventually sacrifices.

### Huli / Jalp
Kedu master who arrived in Tulo a decade before the story begins. Raised Nezha from orphancy. Trained Lanu. Murdered the previous Tulo master to create the vacancy. Has been alive for an indeterminate number of generations by practicing kakari — killing bashi to extend his life. The lian would detect him if he stayed in one place too long, so he keeps moving, engineering openings. Discovered kakari the night before leading others into the valley — understood what the bashi were, what killing them would give him, and fled before he could be detected. The Order of Jalp was built on his absence. He has been correcting the record ever since, in his own favor.

**Misdirection note:** In the prologue, the planted line: *The Order of Jalp was built on that proof — and Jalp, it is said, never returned to correct it.*

### Koya
Temple leader in Estopio. Nezha's love. Killed by Huli during the reveal. Healed by the bashi at the cost of Nezha's ability to hear them.

### Syca
Kaol's best friend. Already compromised by the Sakoku Society before the story begins. Plants the seed in the valley when Kaol refuses. Loses everything when Kaol dismantles the Ring families. Disappears without a confrontation. Book two territory.

### Eflo
Bashi paired with the headmaster of Estopio's training school. Spends most of her time at the sick hall in the Spokes. Kaol's moral compass. Not interested in the institution's politics. The most useful person in the city.

### Hachi
The bashi who pairs with Lanu in the valley. Stays with her after the memory sacrifice. A bashi choosing to remain with someone who can no longer remember why the bond exists is its own kind of answer about what the bond actually is.

## Magic System

**Junsi** — the ability to send one's presence away from the body while remaining tethered to the self. If you can connect your presence to a kedu tree, the tree can do extraordinary things — medicine, communication across the network, manipulation of the sap and bark.

**The bashi** — the physical embodiment of the kedu tree's spirit. Vulpine creatures. Not animals — they are the fruit of the kedu, revealed late in the book. Hearing the bashi is a congenital gift. Without the bashi to guide you back, connecting to the tree risks losing yourself in its overwhelming presence. The bashi is the difference between magic and madness.

**The ceremony price** — every use of the magic costs something the practitioner names. The bashi and tree accept or reject the offer as a verdict on whether the practitioner understands themselves. Jalp's corruption of the system begins here — he named a price in bad faith from the start.

**Kakari** — the practice of killing a bashi to extend one's life. Can be done multiple times to the same bashi, each time producing a rebirth without memory. Jalp discovered this and has been practicing it for generations.

**Lian** — the connectedness of the bashi network. Jalp's presence in the lian is how he finds Enki. It is also what turns against him at the end.

**The cost of overuse** — sanity. Enki pays incrementally, each time telling himself it's worth it. By the end he has what he wanted and isn't capable of appreciating it.

## World

**Tulo** — small keechad town along the Stone Path. Home to Lanu, Nezha, Huli, Nelo. The Kedu tree in the central courtyard. The keechad marshes surrounding it. Where the book begins and ends.

**The Keechad** — the marshlands. The pool of Fero and Naru's tears, according to legend. Lanu's home ground.

**Estopio** — the capital. Built around the Great Kedu tree. Divided into the Rings (wealthy families), the Spokes (middle class), the Hub (center), and the Quag (the poor, built on ground too soggy for stone). The Quag has no plumbing — the river running through it is not just water. The bridges across it are named the Father and the Mother, after the mountains watching over the city.

**The Rings** — wealthy families who control the kedu trade. The J'Rasa family is among them. The coppicing of kedu trees — deliberately stunted to produce more harvestable material — keeps the trees from reaching their full connection to the lian. The trees are being used against themselves.

**The Order of Jalp** — the religious institution built on Jalp's founding myth. Has been captured by the Ring families from the inside.

**The Jalp Valley / Valley of the Mother and Father** — where the Great Kedu grows. Site of the New Light Ceremony. Where Jalp first went alone and came back changed.

**The New Light Ceremony (NLC)** — annual rite of passage for new magic practitioners. Candidates enter the valley, name their price, and may be paired with a bashi. Has become the mechanism by which the institution controls access to magic.

**The Stone Path** — the main route connecting Tulo, Maka, and Estopio.

**The Sakoku Society** — a secretive group dedicated to destroying the Great Kedu and restoring the old ways — stone over keechad, the mountains' spirits returned. They have the core of Utga, the mountain whose tantrum froze the world. Book two territory.

## Prologue

The official history. What the Order of Jalp teaches. What every child in Estopio grows up believing. Written in the register of received history — not lived experience.

Short. Five sentences. The planted line:

*The Order of Jalp was built on that proof — and Jalp, it is said, never returned to correct it.*

Reads on first pass as a footnote about his mysterious disappearance. Reads on second pass as the first lie.

## Part One — Tulo

**Lanu POV**
Morning in the keechad. Lanu and Nelo's last conversation — the most important scene in the book. Nelo sends her to the marsh deliberately, though Lanu doesn't know it. The farewell the reader will carry for the entire novel.

**Nezha POV**
Her presence wandering the marshes during morning practice. The screams. The agonizing seconds of reunion before she can move. She finds Nelo dead, Lanu unconscious at the base of the Kedu tree. She holds Tulo together while Lanu recovers. Huli's room is empty and neat.

**Lanu POV**
Lanu wakes. Nezha tells her. The grief is private — no one else in Tulo could hear Nelo. Huli returns. Measured, sorrowful, controlled. Presents going to Estopio as necessity and tradition. It is neither. It is exactly what he needed.

---

## Interlude — Estopio, Generations Past (I)

Jalp ventures into the valley alone. Returns with stories. Returns again with a bashi. The Rasa family. The New Light Ceremony established. Jalp leads others toward the valley — and disappears the night before they meet the bashi. The others receive their bashi. The city grows rich. The legend calcifies.

*What actually happened:* Jalp discovered kakari the night before. Understood what the bashi were. Fled before the lian could detect him. Has been moving ever since.

---

## Part Two — The Journey

**Nezha POV**
The Stone Path. The scale of what Nelo's death has severed. Bandits attack. Nezha is injured. They reach Estopio and go directly to the temple.

**Nezha POV**
The temple. Treatment. Koya. Something begins quietly.

---

## Interlude — Estopio, Earlier Generation (II)

The Rasa family consolidates power. The Rings emerge. The Quag deepens. The Order of Jalp becomes the mechanism of control. The institution Lanu is walking into has been designed this way for generations.

---

## Part Three — Estopio

**Enki POV**
Assigned to temple duty. Meets Lanu. Shows her the city — the Rings, the Spokes, the Quag. His sister's story. She had the gift, connected to the Great Kedu without a bashi, lost herself. Alive but unreachable.

*Misdirection:* He passes a woman struggling with a child and a heavy basket, thinks briefly he should help, keeps moving.

**Kaol POV**
Below the Rings. The Sakoku Society. The open flame burning kedu wood. The ask. The threat. He doesn't answer. The sick hall. Eflo. Yu. Syca — the realization.

**Lanu and Enki — alternating**
The coppiced trees. The institution's self-serving architecture. The kiss.

**Nezha POV**
Koya showing her the temple's internal corruption. Nezha beginning to understand that Huli sent them here knowing what they'd find.

---

## Interlude — Tulo, A Decade Past (III)

Huli arrives in Tulo. The previous master recently dead — poisoned, not ruled as such. Nelo has to accept him or the tree dies. She accepts. She watches him for ten years and can tell exactly two people. One is the source. One is a child she is quietly preparing.

---

## Part Four — The New Light Ceremony

**Multiple POV**
The candidates name their prices. Enki names something small. Kaol names something genuine. Lanu names something that costs her.

Kaol doesn't plant the seed. Syca, watching, understands. The seed goes into Syca's pocket. We see him plant it — a short, quiet scene. One image that sticks. The reader registers it without understanding it yet.

**Lanu POV**
The valley. The fog storm. Alone. The junsi comes naturally — the keechad was practice for this. The bashi find her. They reveal kakari, the corruption, Jalp's long game. They pair her with Hachi. She returns to Estopio having understood something no one else knows.

**Enki POV**
Pushing further than he named. The voices overwhelming. Something finds him — a voice that knows exactly what he wants. Offers him his sister. He thinks he found an ally. He comes back without a bashi. Changed.

**Kaol POV**
Paired with a bashi. Eflo is not surprised.

---

## Part Five — The Unraveling

**Lanu POV**
Confronts the Order with what the bashi told her. The bashi in the city move. They were on her side.

**Enki POV**
Joins the confrontation. His methods diverge. He kills the Rasa family leader. The cause is just. The act is wrong. The voice in the lian has been directing his anger.

**Kaol POV**
Steps away from his family's position publicly. Provides the testimony. The Ring families are exposed and dismantled. Syca's family loses everything. Syca disappears without a confrontation.

**Nezha POV**
The temple votes Nezha as the new Tenzo. She accepts. Koya beside her.

---

## Part Six — Jalp

**Lanu POV**
Huli steps into the courtyard. Lanu thinks he has come to help. Hachi knows before she does. The reveal. The decade in Tulo. The poisoned master. Nelo's silence. All of it landing at once.

He kills Koya.

**Nezha POV**
She asks the bashi. The price. She gives up the ability to hear them — without hesitation. Koya lives. Nezha steps back from the Tenzo role. She cannot lead what she just gave away.

**Enki POV**
Steps in. Hachi cannot guide him properly — the costs accumulated past the point of return. The voices take him in the middle of trying to do something right. He gets what he wanted. He is not quite himself anymore.

*His sister:* Jalp's promise was hollow. Whether she recovers or remains lost — a single line in the denouement closes this. Enki's arc was built on a lie he chose to believe.

**Lanu POV**
Turns the lian against Jalp. The sacrifice — her memory of Nelo. Everything Nelo carried alone. The morning she sent Lanu to the marsh. The ears folding over. Gone.

Jalp, cut off from the lian, ages. He doesn't get a confrontation. He just ends.

Lanu stands in the courtyard with no memory of why any of this mattered personally. She knows the facts. She doesn't feel them. Hachi stays.

---

## Denouement

The city votes. The old ways barely lose. New lines drawn. The Rings opened. The wall to the Quag removed. A council of bashi and people established.

**Lanu** — refuses leadership. Goes back to Tulo. A city that needs a kedu master, a tree that has been without one long enough, a keechad she knows better than anywhere else. She can still hear bashi. She can't remember the one who prepared her for all of it.

**Kaol** — stays in Estopio. Sets up a sick hall in the Rings. Eflo comes to help. Yu is there, older, learning to talk.

**Nezha and Koya** — go home to Tulo with Lanu. Nezha tends the tree. She can't hear the bashi anymore. She doesn't need to.

**Enki** — has what he wanted. The system has shifted. He is not quite himself anymore. *[Ending image TBD — needs a specific final scene.]*

**Syca** — gone. The seed is in the ground. Book two.

The wind moves through the passes at night, still looking for the rest of the tribe.

## Notes on Structure

**The prologue** should be short — five sentences of received history, written in the register of official record. Not the legend of Fero and Naru (that is geography, not story — it belongs embedded in the narrative, perhaps at the New Light Ceremony). The Jalp prologue plants the flag that pays off at the end.

**The legend of Fero and Naru** belongs inside the narrative. A character recites it, or it appears carved somewhere, or it is told at the New Light Ceremony. Embedded mythology lands harder than prefaced mythology.

**The historical interludes** need a consistent voice — possibly the format of official record, decree, or institutional history, which reinforces that history in this world is official and therefore unreliable.

**Lanu and Nelo's farewell** is the most important scene in the book. The reader carries it forward through everything. When the memory sacrifice lands at the end, that morning is what they flash back to. It needs to be perfect.

**Enki's opening** establishes the misdirection. He is charming, funny, resourceful, sympathetic — and entirely self-oriented. The planted sentence does the work quietly. On first read: relatable. On second read: this is exactly who Enki is.
